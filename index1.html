Message Content Development 30 mins

  • Craft concise messaging
  • Develop clear CTAs
  • Create accessible language
+

Key Terms

  • Message framing - How information is presented to influence perception and decision-making
  • Gain framing - Emphasizing benefits of taking action vs. loss framing (consequences of inaction)
  • Health literacy - Ability to understand and use health information to make decisions
  • Call to action (CTA) - Clear directive that prompts immediate response
  • Barrier-addressing language - Proactively countering common objections or concerns

Discussion Points

  • How can we frame sensitive health topics in ways that reduce stigma?
  • What reading level is appropriate for our target audience?
  • How do we balance comprehensive information with brevity?
  • What language choices might unintentionally create barriers?
  • How can messaging address emotional barriers like fear or embarrassment?

Resource Card Creation & Visual Design 75 mins

  • Develop focused resource cards with strong visual design
  • Highlight key services using effective layout
  • Create visually compelling materials with QR codes
+

Key Terms

  • Visual hierarchy - Organizing elements to guide attention to most important information
  • White space - Strategic use of empty space to enhance readability and focus
  • Color psychology - Using color strategically to evoke specific emotions or meanings
  • Typography pairing - Combining complementary fonts for headings and body text
  • Information chunking - Grouping related information for easier processing
  • Information hierarchy - Organization of content based on importance and visual emphasis
  • User journey - The path a teen would take from noticing the card to accessing services
  • Barrier reduction - Proactively addressing obstacles that prevent service utilization

Discussion Points

  • What information is most critical for teens to know about each resource type?
  • How can we emphasize confidentiality protections in a reassuring way?
  • What design approaches make resource information less intimidating?
  • How can resource cards serve as conversation starters for peer educators?
  • How do design choices impact the way teens perceive health information?
  • What visual elements might reduce stigma around sensitive topics?
  • How might different visual styles resonate with different teen demographics?

Activity

Part 1: Design Framework & Content Planning (25 mins)

  • Divide into three specialist teams (STI testing, food programs, mental health services)
  • Each team establishes visual design principles:
    • Select a primary and secondary color palette that evokes appropriate emotions
    • Choose complementary typography for headings and body text
    • Create visual hierarchy template (what information goes where)
    • Select appropriate visual elements (icons, graphics, photos) that reflect youth culture
  • Each team reviews their section of the Teen Survival Guide and:
    • Identifies 2-3 most critical services to highlight based on accessibility and relevance
    • Creates a content outline including:
      • An attention-grabbing headline addressing a primary need or concern
      • Bulleted key information (location, hours, eligibility, cost)
      • Transportation options and accessibility details
      • Confidentiality protections and parental involvement requirements
      • A clear "next steps" section with simple instructions
    • Develops a specific "barrier-busting" message that addresses the primary concern teens might have
  • Create a quick style guide to ensure consistency across all materials

Part 2: Card Design and Creation (35 mins)

  • Using design templates and materials provided:
    • Create visually distinct resource cards for each category using appropriate color coding
    • Apply consistent branding while differentiating by service type
    • Implement effective information hierarchy with clear visual organization
    • Include compelling imagery that is inclusive and non-stigmatizing
    • Add QR code placement with clear instructions for what scanning the code will provide
  • Focus on creating cards that feature:
    • Clear, compelling headline that addresses a need or pain point
    • Visually distinct sections with essential service information
    • Key details highlighted (cost, confidentiality, transportation options)
    • QR code with brief description of where it leads
    • Appropriate imagery that is inclusive and non-stereotyping
    • Contact information formatted for easy reference
  • Apply information architecture principles:
    • Most important information at top/left (eye tracking patterns)
    • Group related information with visual cues
    • Use bullets or numbered lists for sequential steps
    • Incorporate white space strategically to prevent overwhelm

Part 3: Peer Testing and Refinement (15 mins)

  • Conduct rapid-feedback sessions where teams exchange cards and evaluate:
    • First impression: What's your immediate takeaway about this resource?
    • Navigation: How easily can you find the most important information?
    • Clarity: Is there anything confusing or missing?
    • Appeal: Would you pick this up? Would you keep it?
    • Action: Does it make you more likely to use the service?
    • Accessibility: Is text readable? Do colors provide sufficient contrast?
  • Make quick refinements based on feedback
  • Finalize production-ready versions with any necessary improvements

Reflection Questions

  • How does your resource card address both practical and emotional barriers to access?
  • What feedback surprised you during the peer testing phase?
  • How do your cards balance between providing enough information and being overwhelmingly detailed?
  • What design elements might help overcome identified barriers to accessing services?
  • How have you balanced creativity with clarity in your design choices?
  • What considerations went into making your materials appealing across different cultural backgrounds?
