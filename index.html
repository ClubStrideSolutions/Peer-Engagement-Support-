Resource Card Creation & Visual Design Completion 40 mins

  • Incorporate peer feedback
  • Finalize all design elements
  • Quality check & production prep
+

Key Terms

  • Production-ready - Materials finalized with proper resolution, bleeds, and formatting for printing
  • Accessibility check - Ensuring materials meet standards for readability and inclusive design
  • Version control - System for tracking changes and maintaining final approved versions
  • Print specifications - Technical requirements including size, color mode, and file format

Activity

Part 1: Peer Feedback Integration (15 mins)

  • Review feedback collected from Session 4
  • Prioritize changes based on:
    • Clarity improvements
    • Accessibility enhancements
    • Youth appeal factors
    • Barrier-reduction effectiveness
  • Make final design refinements

Part 2: Production Preparation (20 mins)

  • Finalize all resource cards ensuring:
    • Consistent branding across all materials
    • QR codes tested and functional
    • Contact information verified
    • Print specifications met (300 DPI, CMYK color mode)
    • Bleed margins added where needed
  • Create final checklist including:
    • All text proofread for errors
    • Images high-resolution and properly licensed
    • Colors accessible (contrast ratios checked)
    • File naming convention followed

Part 3: Quality Assurance (5 mins)

  • Partner review using production checklist
  • Test print one sample of each design
  • Final approval and file submission

Reflection Questions

  • What feedback most improved your final design?
  • How did you balance creativity with practical constraints?
  • What would you do differently if starting over?

Prepare School Distribution Strategy 30 mins

  • Map school distribution locations
  • Assign team roles for school coverage
  • Create tracking system for peer-to-peer sharing
+

Key Terms

  • Barrier reduction - Proactively addressing obstacles that prevent students from accessing health services
  • Peer influence - Leveraging trusted relationships between students to share important health information
  • School-based outreach - Distribution and engagement activities conducted within the high school setting
  • Grade-level targeting - Tailoring messages and approaches for different student year groups

Discussion Points

  • Where do students naturally gather and feel comfortable in your school?
  • What times during the school day maximize peer-to-peer interaction?
  • How can we reach different student groups (freshmen vs. seniors, different social circles)?
  • What school policies should guide our distribution approach?

Activity

⚠️ Important: The strategy you create will be implemented in your high schools starting tomorrow. Ensure all team members understand their roles and have access to materials before leaving class today.

  • Safety and supervision needs
  • Complementary services available

    • Part 2: Role Assignment & Accountability (10 mins)

    • Create Distribution Team Roles:
      • Zone Leaders: Responsible for specific areas of the school (e.g., different floors, buildings, or wings)
      • Engagement Specialists: Lead peer conversations during lunch, passing periods, and after school
      • Data Trackers: Record distribution numbers and capture peer feedback
      • Supply Managers: Store materials safely at school and manage daily distribution kits
      • School Liaison: Coordinate with administration, teachers, and school wellness staff
    • Develop accountability structure:
      • Morning huddles before first period
      • Lunchtime check-ins for progress updates
      • After-school debrief sessions
      • Shared digital tracking via school-approved platforms

    Part 3: Tracking System Creation (10 mins)

    • Design Distribution Tracking Document including:
      • Date, time, and location fields
      • Number of cards distributed by type
      • Estimated reach (people engaged)
      • Notable interactions or feedback section
      • Restock needs tracker
      • Team member responsible field
    • Create engagement metrics section:
      • Peer conversations by grade level
      • Questions answered by topic area
      • Student-to-student referrals tracked
      • Interest in follow-up information
    • Establish daily reporting process:
      • Set after-school submission deadline for team members
      • Share tracking document via school-approved platforms
      • Test the tracking system with a practice entry
      • Ensure site lead has access to compile school data

    School-Based Outreach Launch & Metric Tracking Week 3 Implementation

    • Execute distribution plan within your high schools
    • Engage peers during school hours
    • Site leads compile and submit daily school metrics

    School-Based Implementation:

    Using the distribution strategy and role assignments created today, begin outreach activities in your high schools starting tomorrow morning. Each team member should arrive prepared with materials and clear understanding of their coverage areas within the school.

    Role-Specific Execution:

    • Zone Leaders: Cover your assigned school areas during peak traffic times (passing periods, lunch, before/after school)
    • Engagement Specialists: Position yourself in high-traffic areas to have natural peer conversations
    • Data Trackers: Move throughout the school to capture real-time metrics and feedback
    • Supply Managers: Set up distribution stations and ensure materials are accessible throughout the day
    • School Liaison: Check in with administration and coordinate with teachers who support the initiative

    Daily Metrics to Track (Using Your Tracking System):

    • Cards distributed per school location (cafeteria, hallways, library, etc.)
    • Grade levels reached (freshman, sophomore, junior, senior)
    • Peak engagement times during the school day
    • Common questions from different student groups
    • Teacher or staff support received
    • Peer-to-peer referrals (students sharing with friends)

    Site Lead Daily Responsibilities:

    1. Collect tracking sheets from all team members by end of school day
    2. Compile data from all participating high schools into master tracking document
    3. Analyze metrics against the KPIs established in previous sessions
    4. Submit consolidated report via Google Classroom by 7:00 PM daily including:
      • Quantitative metrics from each school's tracking sheets
      • Qualitative insights about peer responses and engagement
      • Photos documenting distribution activities (with appropriate permissions)
      • Challenges faced within school settings and adjustments made
      • Coordination needs between different school teams

    Success Indicators (Based on Your SMART Goals):

    • Reaching students across all grade levels
    • Coverage of key school locations during peak times
    • Quality of peer-to-peer conversations (not just handouts)
    • Positive feedback from school staff and administration
    • Evidence of students sharing resources with friends
    • Increased awareness demonstrated through follow-up questions

    ⚡ Reminder: Distribution begins in your high schools tomorrow morning. Follow your distribution strategy while remaining flexible to adapt based on real-time peer feedback. Site leads must submit the first daily report by 7:00 PM tomorrow after the first day of distribution.
